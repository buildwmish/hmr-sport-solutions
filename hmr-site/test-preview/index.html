When to bring me in

Bring me in when:

  • the direction is not yet settled, but momentum is building
  • options are contested and stakeholders are not yet aligned
  • the investment case is forming, but the logic needs testing
  • there is pressure to move, but readiness is still unclear
  • the consequences of getting the next step wrong are material

This is usually the point where independent advice adds most value: when the decision is live, the pressure is real, and the cost of a poor call will show up later.

What I actually do

I provide independent advice when organisations need to decide what to do, why it stacks up, and whether the timing is right.

The work usually involves clarifying the real decision, testing assumptions and investment logic, working through stakeholder complexity, and assessing sequencing, readiness and risk.

I am brought in early enough to question the brief, sharpen the case, and reduce the risk of premature lock-in.

About

My experience spans Sport New Zealand, Auckland Council, Tātaki Auckland Unlimited, the University of Auckland and Aktive.

Across those environments, the pattern has been consistent: important decisions are often made while assumptions are still weak, stakeholders are not fully aligned, and pressure to progress is stronger than the case for doing so.

That is usually where I add most value.

I now work independently, helping organisations make better decisions earlier, before commitments harden and before avoidable complexity becomes someone else’s problem.
